## Overview

This page is less about a direct line to my current work and more about **how I became a researcher** in the first place.

My early experience in **marine biology** helped build the habits that still shape my work: **careful observation**, comfort with **unfamiliar systems**, respect for **data quality**, and **curiosity-driven** problem solving. Even though I ultimately moved toward neuroscience and machine learning, this kind of field and biology work mattered because it trained the same underlying research instincts.

## Why it matters

I like including this project because it shows **range**. My path was not perfectly linear, and I think that is a strength. I have always been drawn to **complex systems**, **challenging environments**, and questions that require both **patience** and **experimentation**.
