## Overview

I developed and deployed a **web-based annotation platform** to support flexible labeling of naturalistic movie stimuli for downstream neuroimaging, behavioral, and machine learning research.

The goal was straightforward: make it easier for researchers to generate **structured labels** from rich audiovisual stimuli without relying on brittle one-off workflows. The result was a practical internal tool that supported repeated use across projects and collaborators.

## What it supported

- **Manual labeling** of naturalistic movie stimuli  
- **Flexible annotation workflows** for different projects  
- **Structured outputs** for downstream modeling and analysis  
- **Collaboration** across lab members and partner institutions  

## Why it matters

This project is less about flashy modeling and more about **research infrastructure**. It shows that I can build tools that other people actually use, standardize messy workflows, and improve the quality and reusability of downstream data.

## My role

Designed and implemented the platform, supported **deployment and usage**, and helped integrate the annotations into later neuroimaging, behavioral, and ML workflows.
